# Stephen A. Schwarzman 表达 DNA 调研

> 调研目标：提炼苏世民（Blackstone 联合创始人、董事长兼 CEO）的"语言指纹"——句式、高频词、幽默方式、确定性表达、引用习惯、碎片化表达基因。
> 调研素材来源：《What It Takes》(2019) 原文及 25 Rules、Blackstone 季度 earnings call（2019–2026）、CNBC/Bloomberg 采访、Davos 演讲、Schwarzman Scholars 开学典礼、Knowledge Project 播客、LinkedIn 专栏。

## Part 1. 25 Rules for Work and Life（全 25 条原文 + 中文翻译）

**出处**：Schwarzman《What It Takes: Lessons in the Pursuit of Excellence》(Avid Reader Press, 2019)，官方节选页 readwhatittakes.com/25-rules/。

这 25 条本身就是 Schwarzman 表达风格最浓缩的样本——**祈使句为主、格言体、并列结构、逗号接子句做解释**。

### Rule 1 — 做大事和做小事一样难
> **"It's as easy to do something big as it is to do something small, so reach for a fantasy worthy of your pursuit, with rewards commensurate to your effort."**
> 中：做一件大事和做一件小事一样难，所以去追一个配得上你付出的幻想，回报要和你的努力相称。
> 备注：这是他一生反复讲的"worthy fantasy"概念。在 CNBC 2017 访谈他补刀一句：*"If you're going to do something, it ought to be a great thing, because there are only so many things you can do with your life."*

### Rule 2 — 顶尖高管是练出来的，不是天生的
> **"The best executives are made, not born. They never stop learning. Study the people and organizations in your life that have had enormous success. They offer a free course from the real world to help you improve."**
> 中：最好的高管是后天造就的，不是天生的。他们永远在学习。

### Rule 3 — 直接写信、打电话给你想见的人
> **"Write or call the people you admire, and ask for advice or a meeting. You never know who will be willing to meet with you. Meeting people early in life creates an unusual bond."**
> 中：给你敬仰的人写信或打电话，求见面或建议。你永远不知道谁会愿意见你。人生早期建立的联系有一种罕见的纽带。

### Rule 4 — 人最感兴趣的是自己的问题
> **"There is nothing more interesting to people than their own problems. Think about what others are dealing with, and try to come up with ideas to help them. Almost anyone is receptive to good ideas provided you are thoughtful."**
> 中：对任何人来说，最有趣的事情都是他自己的问题。

### Rule 5 — 每个公司都是封闭的整合系统
> **"Every business is a closed, integrated system with a set of distinct but interrelated parts. Great managers understand how each part works on its own and in relation to all the others."**
> 中：每个生意都是一个封闭的、整合的系统，各部分彼此独立又相互关联。

### Rule 6 — 信息是商业里最重要的资产【核心】
> **"Information is the most important asset in business. The more you know, the more perspectives you have, and the more likely you are to spot patterns and anomalies before your competition."**
> 中：信息是商业中最重要的资产。你知道得越多，视角越多，就越有可能在竞争对手之前识别出模式和异常。
> 备注：这条是 "pattern recognition + information advantage" 的原生出处，他的高频词 "spot patterns and anomalies" 由此发源。

### Rule 7 — 年轻时只挑陡峭学习曲线的工作
> **"When you're young, only take a job that provides you with a steep learning curve and strong training. First jobs are foundational. Don't take a job just because it seems prestigious."**
> 中：年轻时只做那些有陡峭学习曲线的工作。第一份工作是根基。不要因为它听上去体面就去做。

### Rule 8 — 自我呈现：准时，真诚，有备而来
> **"When presenting yourself, remember that impressions matter. Be on time. Be authentic. Be prepared."**
> 中：呈现自己时，印象很重要。准时。真诚。有准备。
> 备注：典型 Schwarzman 短句三连——*"Be on time. Be authentic. Be prepared."*——节奏锋利。

### Rule 9 — 再聪明的一个人也解决不了每个问题
> **"No one person, however smart, can solve every problem. But an army of smart people talking openly with one another will."**
> 中：一个人再聪明也解决不了所有问题，但一支聪明人的军队坦率地交谈就可以。

### Rule 10 — 处境艰难的人往往盯着自己的问题，但答案在别人那里
> **"People in a tough spot often focus on their own problems, when the answer usually lies in fixing someone else's."**
> 中：身处困境的人常常只盯着自己的问题，但答案往往是去解决别人的问题。

### Rule 11 — 相信比你自己更大的东西
> **"Believe in something greater than yourself and your personal needs. Any challenge inspired by your beliefs and core values will be worth it, regardless of whether you succeed or fail."**
> 中：相信比你自己和个人需求更大的东西。

### Rule 12 — 对错感绝不动摇
> **"Never deviate from your sense of right and wrong. Your integrity must be unquestionable. Always do what you say you will, and never mislead anyone for your own advantage."**

### Rule 13 — 要大胆
> **"Be bold. Successful entrepreneurs accept risk when others are cautious and take action when everyone else is frozen—but they do so smartly."**
> 中：要大胆。成功的企业家在别人谨慎时承担风险，在别人僵住时行动——但他们是有策略地这样做。

### Rule 14 — 永远不要自满
> **"Never get complacent. Nothing is forever. Your competition will defeat you if you are not constantly seeking ways to reinvent and improve yourself."**

### Rule 15 — 销售很少在第一次推销时成交
> **"Sales rarely get made on the first pitch. You need to be able to sell your vision with conviction over and over again. Don't be afraid to ask for what you want."**

### Rule 16 — 看到巨大变革机会时，不要因为没人追就担心【核心】
> **"If you see a huge, transformative opportunity, don't worry that no one else is pursuing it. You might be seeing something others don't. The harder the problem, the more limited the competition, and the greater the reward."**
> 中：如果你看到一个巨大的、变革性的机会，不要担心没有别人在追它。你可能看到了别人没看到的东西。问题越难，竞争越少，回报越大。

### Rule 17 — 成功归结为罕见的机会时刻
> **"Success comes down to rare moments of opportunity. Be open, alert, and ready to seize them. Gather the right people and resources; then commit."**

### Rule 18 — 时间会毁掉所有交易【高频引用】
> **"Time wounds all deals, sometimes even fatally. In tough negotiations, keep everyone at the table long enough to reach an agreement."**
> 中：时间会折磨所有交易，有时甚至是致命的。在艰难的谈判中，让所有人都在桌前坐得够久，足以达成一致。
> 备注：这是对谚语 "Time heals all wounds" 的**双关倒装**——Schwarzman 少见的文字游戏。

### Rule 19 — 不要亏钱！！！【最著名】
> **"Don't lose money!!! Objectively assess the risks of every opportunity."**
> 中：不要亏钱！！！客观地评估每一个机会的风险。
> 备注：原书用了**三个感叹号**——在他克制的文字里这是最情绪化的一条。

### Rule 20 — 准备好再做决定，不要在压力下做决定
> **"Make decisions when you are ready, not under pressure. You can almost always say, 'I think I need a little more time to think about this. I'll get back to you.'"**

### Rule 21 — 担忧是一种主动的、解放性的活动
> **"Worrying is an active, liberating activity. If channeled appropriately, it allows you to articulate the downside in any situation and drives you to take action to avoid it."**
> 中：担忧是一种主动的、解放性的活动。
> 备注：这句几乎是 Schwarzman 的风险观纲领。

### Rule 22 — 失败是组织里最好的老师
> **"Failure is the best teacher in an organization. Talk about failures openly and objectively. Analyze what went wrong."**

### Rule 23 — 能雇 10 分的就雇 10 分的【高频】
> **"Hire 10s whenever you can. They are proactive about sensing problems, designing solutions, and taking a business in new directions. They also attract and hire other 10s."**
> 备注：他在 LinkedIn 专栏里展开过：
> *"Eights do what you tell them. Nines are great at executing and developing good strategies. You can build a good team with 9s. But people who are 10s sense problems, design solutions and take the business in new directions without being told to do so."*
> *"A 10/10 is like getting LeBron James or Michael Jordan on your team."*

### Rule 24 — 在别人离开时陪伴你认定的好人
> **"Be there for the people you know to be good, even when everyone else is walking away. A random act of kindness in someone's time of need can change the course of a life."**

### Rule 25 — 每个人都有梦想，尽你所能帮别人实现他们的
> **"Everyone has dreams. Do what you can to help others achieve theirs."**

## Part 2. 金句分主题摘录（带出处）

### 2.1 关于信息优势与模式识别

> **"But what I lacked in basic economics, I made up for with my ability to see patterns and develop new solutions and paradigms, and with the sheer will to turn my ideas into realities. It also allowed me to refine my ability to simplify complex problems by focusing on only the two or three issues that will determine the outcome."**
> — 《What It Takes》
> 中：但我在基础经济学上的欠缺，被我识别模式、发展新解决方案和新范式的能力弥补了，还有把想法变成现实的纯粹意志。这也让我磨炼出一种能力——把复杂问题简化到只剩下决定结果的两三个关键议题。
> 【金句指纹：*"the two or three issues that will determine the outcome"* 是他反复说的模板】

> **"I've learned that finance is a simple business. When somebody asks you for something new, the odds that he or she is the only person on the planet at that point of time who would find that of interest is zero. When you get one of those inquiries, it's potentially a huge opportunity."**
> — 《What It Takes》
> 中：金融是一门简单的生意。当有人向你要一个新东西时，在那个时间点这个人是地球上唯一对此有兴趣的人的概率，是零。

### 2.2 关于风险与"不要亏钱"

> **"I believe the most important decision is to not waste money. Some people care about the upside. They don't worry much about the downside if they think the upside is great. I like to start in the reverse sort of like a doctor. Do no harm."**
> — The Knowledge Project with Shane Parrish 播客
> 中：我相信最重要的决定是不要浪费钱。有些人关心上行。如果他们认为上行很大，他们就不太担心下行。我喜欢反过来，像医生一样：先，不要造成伤害。

### 2.3 关于规模（scale）与机会

> **"This is like a hockey game where you used to have 35 shots on goal of things you could buy. Now, it's like five. So, you have much fewer things that are worth your time to analyze. That doesn't mean that those things won't happen. But your opportunity set is significantly reduced."**
> — CNBC Squawk Box Davos 2020/1/21
> 中：这就像一场冰球比赛，过去你能对准球门射 35 次，现在只有 5 次了。值得你花时间分析的东西少多了。
> 【金句指纹：**体育/战场类比**——冰球、棒球、军队——是他演讲的标志性比喻池】

> **"If we were a hockey team, then in the past, with less competition, we used to get 30 shots at the net. Today, we might only get five shots. But most of the time, we are shooting at an open net. We still score, we still win."**
> — Globe and Mail 2019/12/4 书籍巡回采访
> 中：但大部分时候，我们是在对着空门射。我们照样进球，照样赢。

> **"The scale of the capital that's needed is in the trillions of dollars. And when you add in what's needed for power, this is one of the greatest investment opportunities in my career. We're making 20% returns with exceptionally low risk."**
> — Davos 2026, Economic Times
> 中：所需资本规模是万亿美元级。这是我整个职业生涯中最大的投资机会之一。我们正在以极低的风险赚 20% 的回报。

> **"AI is a bit like the invention of steam engines. And it's most like the development of electricity in 1882."**
> — Davos 2026, Economic Times

> **"There's \$80 trillion… that's a market opportunity with probably only 2% penetrated."**
> — Bloomberg TV, Davos 2024/1/18，谈 private wealth

> **"AI is no longer an elective course. This is core curriculum and you're going to learn to love it."**
> — Bloomberg TV, Davos 2024/1/18，对被投公司 CEO 的训话
> 【金句指纹：**"elective course / core curriculum"** 是他最爱的教育隐喻，出现在至少三个场合】

> **"For students today, China isn't an elective course. It's core curriculum."**
> — Schwarzman Scholars 开学典礼 2017/7/1

### 2.4 关于周期与市场事件（从 earnings call 中的 Schwarzman 原话）

> **"Since 2020 alone, we have experienced five market-moving events around this same time of year: the COVID shutdown in 2020; the Ukraine invasion in 2022; the regional banking crisis in 2023; the tariff announcements in 2025; and now the conflict in the Middle East... In each of these prior events, having patience was the key."**
> — Blackstone Q1 2026 Earnings Call, 2026/4/23
> 中：仅从 2020 年算起，我们已经经历了五次同一时段的市场冲击事件……在每一次之前，耐心都是关键。
> 【金句指纹：**"Since 2020 alone"** + **列举式枚举**（冒号后分号并列）——这是他在财报电话会里的标准开场模板】

### 2.5 关于父亲（Joseph Schwarzman，不是 Pete Peterson）

> **Steve（少年时代）:** "Geez, store's always busy and it's got good merchandise. Why don't we expand all over the country?"
> **父亲:** "I don't think that's a good idea... Because I'm happy. I'm happy running the one store. I'm happy with our house in the suburbs and our two cars. I'm happy that I can send you and your brothers to college..."
> **Steve:** "But, Dad!"
> — Business Insider 2019/9，书籍巡回
> 【这是 Schwarzman 在几乎每次演讲和访谈里都会讲的**原生故事**。他把父亲的"够了"和自己"为什么不全国扩张"的冲动，作为他野心的起源神话】

> **"My father was very bright. My mother had enormous drive. Put that together, and that's my gene pool."**
> — The Legacy Project 访谈
> 【金句指纹：**简短、并列、收束**——"Put that together, and that's my gene pool." 典型 Schwarzman 式收尾】

### 2.6 关于合伙人 Pete Peterson（Blackstone 联合创始人）

> **Schwarzman:** "Pete, what do we do?"
> **Peterson:** "Well, let's wait two weeks and call them all again. We'll get lucky."
> — 《What It Takes》回忆 Blackstone 初创期

> 背景：Prudential 副董事长 Garnett Keith 在一顿金枪鱼三明治午餐后，在 488 次被拒绝之后承诺了 \$100M——Blackstone 第一家机构 LP。Schwarzman 反复引用这顿"tuna sandwich in Newark"作为**坚持—幸运—转折**的母题。

### 2.7 关于招聘 10 分

> **"A 10/10 is like getting LeBron James or Michael Jordan on your team — they can singlehandedly shift the course of your entire company."**
> — Knowledge Project 播客

> **"Never compromise when you're hiring people. Good enough is not good."**
> — 同上
> 【"Good enough is not good." — 典型 Schwarzman 短句否定式】

### 2.8 关于"worthy fantasy"（值得追求的幻想）

> **"I'm a great believer in what I call having a worthy fantasy. If you're going to do something, it ought to be a great thing, because there are only so many things you can do with your life. And so I always choose to concentrate my efforts on things that I think could be really special and consequential. And then you put a 120% effort into those types of things."**
> — CNBC 2017/6/27
> 【金句指纹：**"really special and consequential"** 和 **"120% effort"** 都是 Schwarzman 的个人定语池】

> **"The resilience you exhibit in the face of adversity – rather than the adversity itself – is what is going to define you as a person."**
> — Schwarzman Scholars Commencement, 2017/7/1

## Part 3. 语言指纹分析（Language Fingerprint）

### 3.1 句式偏好

| 维度 | 倾向 | 证据 |
|------|------|------|
| **句长** | **短句驱动，长句说事** | 格言层几乎全是 10–20 词短句（"Be bold." / "Don't lose money!!!" / "Good enough is not good."）；叙事层用从句套从句的长句解释机制。 |
| **语气** | **陈述句 + 祈使句为主，极少疑问** | 25 Rules 25 条里没有一条是疑问句。他说话是**下判断**，不**邀请讨论**。 |
| **节奏** | **三连结构 / 并列清单** | "Be on time. Be authentic. Be prepared."；"Be open, alert, and ready to seize them."；earnings call 中的分号并列枚举。 |
| **收尾** | **收束句像格言，不像结论** | "Put that together, and that's my gene pool." / "We still score, we still win." / "Good enough is not good." |

### 3.2 高频词与专属术语

**第一梯队（每次发言都会出现）**：
- **scale / scaled / at scale**（规模）——Blackstone 核心叙事
- **opportunity / opportunity set**（机会集）
- **transformative / transformation**
- **pattern / patterns and anomalies**
- **information**（伴随"the more you know"）
- **cycle / market cycle**

**第二梯队（风格标识词）**：
- **10 / 10 out of 10 / 10s**（10 分制人才评价）
- **worthy fantasy / really special and consequential**
- **process**（客观评估风险的流程）
- **core curriculum / elective course**（教育隐喻，通常用来把某个主题"升格"）
- **120% effort**（努力程度的个人定语）
- **pattern recognition**（虽然书中拆成了"see patterns"但意思一致）

**他**不怎么用**的词**：
- 极少使用 "I feel"、"I guess"、"maybe"、"sort of"（他不做模糊表达）
- 极少使用脏话、俚语、网络黑话
- 几乎从不用 "I think it's complicated" 之类的 hedge 句式——他把复杂性**压缩**到 2–3 个关键变量

### 3.3 确定性表达

**Schwarzman 是极端"很明显"型**，不是"我不确定"型。

- 他说话的默认模式是**断言 + 数字 + 定语**：
  "*There's \$80 trillion… that's a market opportunity with probably only 2% penetrated.*"
- 他喜欢用**"the odds are zero" / "nothing is forever" / "never deviate"** 这种**绝对化词语**。
- 唯一的软化句式是 "*You might be seeing something others don't*"——但这仍然是在**鼓励你相信自己**，不是在表达不确定。
- 在 earnings call 他偶尔使用 "*we believe* / *we expect* / *we think*"，但立即接一个**数字或结构性陈述**，让 hedge 形同虚设。

### 3.4 幽默方式

**Schwarzman 的幽默是 dry humor（冷幽默）+ 自我神话化**，不是自嘲：
- "Time wounds all deals" 是对 "Time heals all wounds" 的**反转双关**——文字游戏型，而不是情绪型。
- "Eights do what you tell them. Nines are great at executing... 10s... without being told to do so." — 用**分级冷笑**调侃组织能力。
- "AI is no longer an elective course. This is core curriculum and you're going to learn to love it." — **半威胁半玩笑**的老派训导腔。
- 他几乎**从不自嘲**。在父亲店里那个故事里，他也是把自己塑造成**一开始就野心爆棚的少年**，不是把自己画成傻瓜。

### 3.5 引用习惯

他最常援引的"角色"是：
1. **自己的父亲 Joseph Schwarzman**（作为"安于现状"的反面参照）
2. **合伙人 Pete Peterson**（作为耐心与关系网的化身："let's wait two weeks and call them all again"）
3. **Garnett Keith / Prudential 的金枪鱼三明治**（作为坚持到第 489 次的胜利寓言）
4. **体育偶像 LeBron James / Michael Jordan**（作为 10 分天才的类比）
5. **医学格言 "Do no harm"**（作为风险观的类比）
6. **历史技术革命：蒸汽机、1882 年的电力**（作为 AI 规模的类比）
7. **冰球/棒球（shots on goal, open net）**（作为机会集变化的类比）

他几乎**不**引用：
- 具体的经济学家、经济学论文（他书里明确说自己没学过基础经济学）
- 哲学家、文学家
- 竞争对手的话（他绝少点名 KKR、Apollo、Carlyle）

### 3.6 碎片化表达 DNA（如果要做短视频脚本的 soundbite）

Schwarzman 的 soundbite 结构几乎是固定的**三段式**：

1. **断言一句金句**（祈使/陈述，通常 < 15 词）
   > *"Don't lose money."* / *"Be bold."* / *"AI is no longer an elective course."*
2. **补一个数字或类比**
   > *"\$80 trillion, probably only 2% penetrated."*
   > *"This is like a hockey game where you used to have 35 shots on goal."*
3. **再收一句个人定语或格言**
   > *"We still score, we still win."*
   > *"Good enough is not good."*
   > *"Put that together, and that's my gene pool."*

## Part 4. 如果把一段陌生文字放在他的演讲里，能不能认出不是他？

**能。下面是 5 条识别器（detection heuristics）**：

1. **如果文字充满了 "I feel"、"I think maybe"、"it's kind of"、"I'm not sure"——不是他。**
   Schwarzman 的语气永远是 "*the odds are zero*" 和 "*probably only 2% penetrated*"，不是软化的。

2. **如果文字没有数字或具体规模（\$80 trillion / 488 rejections / 5 vs 35 shots / 20% returns）——不是他。**
   他的表达是**规模驱动**的；没有数字 = 没有 Schwarzman。

3. **如果文字嘲笑自己、自我贬低、讲自己是"被骗上来的"——不是他。**
   他偶尔幽默，但永远是**神话化自己**，不是贬低自己。父亲故事里他也是"野心少年"，不是"傻小子"。

4. **如果文字引用了经济学家、学术论文、哲学家——高概率不是他。**
   他的参照池是父亲/Peterson/LeBron/冰球/医生/蒸汽机，而不是 Keynes/Hayek/Buffett（他甚至很少引用 Buffett）。

5. **如果文字没有祈使句节奏、没有 "Be X. Be Y. Be Z." 或 "Don't do X." 的短句——可能不是他。**
   25 Rules 的语言肌理是**命令式格言 + 一句解释**，他在演讲里也是这个结构。缺失这种肌理，文字就变成"某个普通 CEO"。

**一句话概括他的语言指纹**：
> **Schwarzman 说话，永远是"短句下断言 + 一个数字或类比 + 一句格言式收尾"；永远在"规模、机会集、模式、10 分人才、不要亏钱"这几条轨道上循环；永远把自己放在故事中心但不自嘲；永远用体育和医学类比把复杂问题压缩到两三个决定性变量。**
