## 2. 五项成功原则（Five Guiding Principles）

最早出现于 2010-12 MIT 讲座，后在 2014 ICM Seoul、2020 Simons Foundation essay、2022 Abel Oslo 几乎原样重述。

### Simons Foundation 官方版（2020-01-22 essay，A 级）

1. **Do something new; don't run with the pack.** "I am not such a fast runner. If I am one of N people all working on the same problem, there is very little chance I will win."
2. **Surround yourself with the smartest people you can find.** "That extends your reach, and terrific people are usually fun to work with."
3. **Be guided by beauty.** "This is obviously true in doing mathematics or writing poetry, but it is also true in fashioning an organization that is running extremely well."
4. **Don't give up easily.** "Some things take much longer than one initially expects. If the goal is worth achieving, just stick with it."
5. **Hope for good luck!**

### 2022 Abel Prize Oslo 强调（A）

> **Be guided by beauty** — "Just as a great theorem can be very beautiful, a company that's really working efficiently can be beautiful."
> **Hope for good luck** — "That's the most important principle."（明确强调最重要）

## 3. "Good luck vs good strategy" 的反复表达

### 一手：TED 2015（A）
> "I made quite a lot of money with pure luck. I mean, I think it was pure luck. It certainly wasn't mathematical modeling."（指 1978 年后最初两年的外汇/商品交易）

### 一手：Alpha Architect transcript（B）
> "No models. No models for the first two years."

### 二手经典："Am I lucky today?"（Institutional Investor 2000）
> "Luck is largely responsible for my reputation for genius. I don't walk into the office in the morning and say, 'Am I smart today?' I walk in and wonder, 'Am I lucky today?'"

**矛盾张力**：Simons 把早年成功归于运气，但又持续强调五原则是**可学的方法论**。两者并列成立——他的世界观是"严密流程 + 承认运气"，不是二选一。

## 4. "Doing something original" 的反复强调

### 2010 MIT（B）
> "I love to do something new... if you're one of N people working on the same problem... I know I'd be last."

### 2022 Abel Oslo（A）
> "Don't run with the pack — do something original."

### 招聘哲学

**2015 TED（A）**：
> "We hire scientists. We don't hire people from Wall Street. We hire people who have done good science."

**2022 Abel Oslo（A）**：
> "You can teach a physicist finance, but you can't teach a finance person physics."

## 5. 拒绝回答的问题（真正的核心往往在这里）

### 现场：2010 MIT 讲座（B）

Q：能不能讲讲你们的 predictive signals？
> "Of course I'm not going to tell you the various predictive signals — Unless... No, I'm definitely not. [Laughter] That's even bigger secrets than those at, down there [the Defense Analysis at Princeton]."

**反转**：拒答之后他把话题转向**文化与组织**——great people、great infrastructure、open environment、全公司利润分红。

### 2015 TED（A）

Anderson 追问分析哪些数据：
> "Everything is grist for the mill — except hem lengths. Weather, annual reports, quarterly reports, historic data itself, volumes, you name it... We take in terabytes of data a day."

"except hem lengths"（裙摆长度除外）是典型 Simons 幽默——以自嘲式排除消解追问。

### 2000 Institutional Investor（B）

请他解释 Chern-Simons 理论。半小时后 Simons 只说：
> "I can't."

### 拒答的统一结构

1. 直接承认不说（无迂回）
2. 用幽默化解压力（laughter）
3. 把话题平移到可以谈的、更抽象的层面（文化、方法论、数据规模）
4. 从不暗示"其实有秘诀但我藏着"——而是让人觉得"秘诀是 process，不是 formula"

## 6. 即兴类比：数学/科学 → 量化投资

### 密码破译 ≈ 市场信号（TED 2015，A）
> "In looking at the data, after a while I realized: it looks like there's some structure here... just the kind of thing we did back at IDA. You design an algorithm — you test it out on a computer. Does it work? Doesn't it work?"

### 效率市场 ≠ 完全效率

**Numberphile 完整版（B）**：
> "Efficient market theory – that's just not true. There are anomalies in data. Even price data."
> "Gradually we found more and more anomalies. None of them were so overwhelming... They have to be subtle anomalies. You put together a collection of these subtle anomalies and they begin to predict very well."

**矛盾保留**：2000 年说市场 "considerably more efficient"，2022 Abel 说 "not very much more efficient"——可能立场真变了，也可能语境不同。两版本并列保留。

### "Einstein 不会毁掉金融市场"（TED 2015）
> "Bringing science into the investing world has improved that world. It's reduced volatility. It's increased liquidity... So I'm not too worried about Einstein going off and starting a hedge fund."

## 7. 改变立场的瞬间

### 基本面交易 → 纯量化

**Alpha Architect 2015（B）**：早期"gut-wrenching"——"You come in one morning, you think you're a genius... The next morning you come in, you feel like a jerk."

**对同事的名言（二手，Zuckerman 2019 转述，C）**：
> "I don't want to have to worry about the market every minute. I want models that will make money while I sleep. A pure system without humans interfering."

### "黑箱"立场的翻转（Zuckerman 转述，C）

> Simons（最初抵制机器学习）: "I can't get comfortable with what this is telling me... It's a black box!"
> Carmona: "Just follow the data, Jim. It's not me, it's the data."
> Ax: "It works, Jim. And it makes rational sense... humans can't forecast prices."

**Simons 从拒绝黑箱 → 最终接受并制度化为铁律**。

### 制度化后的铁律

**SFSU 2014（B）**：
> "The only rule is that we never override the computer. No one ever comes in any day and says the computer wants to do this and that's crazy and we shouldn't do it."

**MIT 2010（B）**：
> "If you're going to trade using models you just slavishly use the models. You do whatever the hell it says no matter how smart or dumb you think it is at that moment, and that turned out to be a wonderful decision."

**张力保留**：他自己当年**曾反对黑箱**，最终**把反对自己那一面的声音定为公司铁律**——"never override the computer"。一次性、不可逆的立场翻转。

## 8. 科学家式的自述框架

### "一生三段"（Abel 2022 短讲，A）
> "I did a lot of math, I made a lot of money, and I gave almost all of it away. That's the story of my life."

math / money / give away——他从不用 "entrepreneur" 或 "investor" 作为自我定位。

### "Machine learning, in a sense"（TED 2015，A）
> "In a certain sense, what we did was machine learning. You look at a lot of data, and you try to simulate different predictive schemes, until you get better and better at it."

2015 年语境下他谨慎地说 "in a certain sense"——不把 Renaissance 与时髦的 ML 炒作绑在一起。

### 数据原始积累（TED 2015）
> "The real thing was to gather a tremendous amount of data... We went down to the Federal Reserve and copied interest rate histories and stuff like that, because it didn't exist on computers."

## 9. 收费与商业边界（TED 2015，A）

> "We charged the highest fees in the world at one time. Five and 44, that's what we charge."（5% flat fee + 44% of profits）

直接承认收费极高，不辩护、不美化。
