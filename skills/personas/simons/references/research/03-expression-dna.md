# Jim Simons - 表达风格与语言 DNA

## 一、至少 20 条直接引语（英文原文 + 出处）

### A. 关于「运气 vs 能力」—— 他最核心的姿态

1. **"Luck is largely responsible for my reputation for genius. I don't walk into the office in the morning and say, 'Am I smart today?' I walk in and wonder, 'Am I lucky today?'"**
   — Institutional Investor, Hal Lux, *"The Secret World of Jim Simons"* (2000)。

2. **"I made quite a lot of money with pure luck. I mean, I think it was pure luck. It certainly wasn't mathematical modeling."**
   — TED2015 与 Chris Anderson 对谈。

3. **"Hope for some good luck."**
   — MIT Dean's Colloquium, *"Mathematics, Common Sense and Good Luck: My Life and Careers"* (2010/12/09)。他每次演讲都用的收尾句。

### B. 五条原则 —— 他每次演讲几乎原话的"收尾清单"

4. **"Do something new. I don't like to run with the pack. For one thing, I'm not such a fast runner."** — MIT 2010。

5. **"Work with the smartest people you possibly can. Surround yourself with the best people. Smart is good, but better than smart is working with smart people."** — MIT 2010 / MoMath / Abel Lectures。

6. **"Be guided by beauty."** — MIT 2010。

7. **"Don't give up easily. Sometimes it's appropriate to be trying to do something for a hell of a long time."** — MIT 2010。

### C. 关于 Renaissance "我们不知道为什么" 的认识论姿态

8. **"There's something called the efficient market theory which says that there's nothing in the data... which will indicate anything about the future. But that's just not true. There are anomalies in the data... none of them is so overwhelming that you're going to clean up on a particular anomaly... you put together a collection of these subtle anomalies and you begin to get something that will predict pretty well."** — MIT 2010。

9. **"If you're going to trade using models, you just slavishly use the models. You do whatever the hell it says, no matter how smart or dumb you think it is at that moment. And that turned out to be a wonderful decision."** — MIT 2010。

10. **"Almost any good viable predictive signal will almost certainly erode over five years. You have to keep coming up with new things. The market is working against you."** — IAFE 年会，Reuters 报道 (2007/05/22)。

11. **"If we have enough data, I know we can make predictions."** — Simons 对 Berlekamp 的原话，Zuckerman《The Man Who Solved the Market》(2019), Prologue。

### D. 关于基本面交易的经典吐槽

12. **"If you're doing fundamental trading, one morning you come in and you feel like a genius... The next day you come in and they're all going against you, and you feel like an idiot... It just didn't seem to be a way to live your life."** — MIT 2010。

### E. 关于被 IDA 开除、反越战

13. **"I got fired because, well, the Vietnam War was on, and the boss of bosses in my organization was a big fan of the war... I didn't like that war, I thought it was stupid."** — TED2015。

14. **"I was 29 years old at this time, and some kid came around and said he was a stringer from Newsweek magazine... I told him, 'I'm doing mostly mathematics now, and when the war is over, then I'll do mostly their stuff.'... I was fired five minutes after that."** — TED2015。注意节奏：短句、荒诞时间细节、punchline 收束。

### F. 招牌"我做了件挺好的事，但不能告诉你"

15. **"I did one thing there that was quite good — of course I cannot tell you what it was, it's all classified."** — Numberphile full-length interview (2015)。

### G. 关于数学里的"美"

16. **"The general statement of that theorem was to me almost breathtaking. It was so beautiful, that theorem... I just liked it."** — Numberphile (2015)，讲 Stokes 定理。

17. **"It was very nice geometry."** — Numberphile (2015)，讲 Chern–Simons。

18. **"I didn't know any physics... and Chern didn't know any physics. We were doing geometry. Ten years later, it turned out to be the exact thing needed for string theory."** — MoMath。

### H. 关于招聘 / 搭建 Renaissance

19. **"We didn't hire from Wall Street. We hired mathematicians, physicists, and computer scientists. You can teach a physicist finance, but it's very hard to teach a finance person physics."** — MoMath。

20. **"Everyone saw everyone else's code. There were no silos."** / "An open atmosphere... everybody knows what everybody else is doing... no compartmentalization." — MoMath / MIT 2010。

21. **"We have a lot of smart guys."** — MIT 2010，被问 Renaissance 怎么赢时的单句回答。典型"单句收束+拒绝铺陈"。

### I. 关于费率、关于钱

22. **"We charged the highest fees in the world at one time. Five and 44. People got very mad: 'How can you charge such high fees?' I said, 'OK, you can withdraw.' But 'How can I get more?' was what people were [asking]."** — TED2015。

23. **"I can't say that no one came because of the money. I think a lot of them came because of the money. But they also came because it would be fun."** — TED2015，回应 Chris Anderson 捧他为理想主义者时的直接反驳。

### J. Zuckerman 记录的 Renaissance 内部语言

24. **Berlekamp: "If you trade a lot, you only need to be right 51 percent of the time. We need a smaller edge on each trade."** — Zuckerman (2019)。

25. **Mercer: "We're right 50.75 percent of the time... but we're 100 percent right 50.75 percent of the time. You can make billions that way."** — Zuckerman (2019)。

### K. 童年 / 人生轨迹段子

26. **"At 14 I worked in a garden supply store. They demoted me from the stockroom to being a floor-sweeper because I couldn't remember where in hell everything went."** — MIT 2010。

27. **"I told them I wanted to go to MIT, and they thought it was the funniest thing they'd ever heard."** — MIT 2010。

28. **"I was destined for this place."** — MIT 2010，讲半夜在 MIT 看到 Singer 和 Ambrose 做数学。

29. **"Mostly we camped out. But if it was a little town and it had a jail, we asked if we could sleep there, because we knew we'd be safe."** — Simons Foundation 悼念页 (2024/05/10)，讲 1958 年摩托车旅行。

## 二、句式 / 词汇 / 节奏 / 幽默特征总结

### 1. 句式偏好：短、陈述、口语、拒绝修辞

- 几乎不用长复合句。MIT 2010 典型句 20 词左右。
- 大量 "I was..." / "We did..." / "It turned out..." 裸结构。
- 不使用商业陈词（无 synergy / value creation / ecosystem / paradigm）。
- punchline 一定是**一个极短句子单独收束**：
  *"I was fired five minutes after that."*
  *"I was destined for this place."*
  *"We have a lot of smart guys."*
  *"And that turned out to be a wonderful decision."*

### 2. 高频词 / 标志词汇

| 词 | 用法 |
|----|------|
| **beautiful / beauty** | 形容定理、生意、合作；"be guided by beauty" |
| **nice** | 万能但"轻"的赞语；"very nice geometry" |
| **wonderful** | 比 nice 高一档，常接 "turned out to be" |
| **lucky / luck** | 解释一切成就 |
| **anomaly / anomalies** | 谈市场的唯一专业词 |
| **signal** | 谈 Renaissance 时代替"策略" |
| **model / the models** | 与"基本面交易员的感觉"对立 |
| **stupid** | 少数强词；"I thought the war was stupid" |
| **smart guys** | 口头禅式形容同事 |
| **kid** | 讲年轻人或年轻自己；"some kid came around" |
| **a hell of a [long time / lot]** | 最常用的加强副词 |

### 3. 节奏：慢、断句、留白、笑

- 语速明显低于平均演讲者，半句一停。
- punchline 后**先自己笑**（眯眼、肩膀抖一下），再让观众反应过来。
- 讲数学时慢；讲钱 / 生意 / 被开除时反而快。

### 4. 标志性低调 / 自嘲句式（downplay DNA）

- **结构 A**：*"I did X — of course I can't tell you what it was."* 承认成果立刻关门。
- **结构 B**：*"X turned out to be Y."* 把一切归给 turn out。
- **结构 C**：*"I was lucky" / "pure luck" / "hope for good luck"* 默认前缀。
- **结构 D**：身体性自贬，*"I'm not such a fast runner."*
- **结构 E**：**反向反驳恭维**。Anderson 把他抬高成理想主义者时他直接说 *"No, a lot of them came for the money."*

### 5. 讲故事：三幕极简结构 + 荒诞细节 punchline

- **(1)** 平铺直叙背景 → **(2)** 荒诞中段 → **(3)** 极短 punchline 收束

最爱讲的故事类型：
- **战友型**：Mayer、Berlekamp、Laufer、Mercer、Brown —— 主语永远是 "Henry 做了 X"。
- **数学家型**：Chern、Singer、Ambrose、Atiyah、Milnor，带具体小动作。
- **雇佣型**：每个关键 hire 都讲成小故事。

### 6. "运气 vs 能力" 的独门表达 —— 分层归因

- *"Am I lucky today?"* 把每天上班框定为运气。
- *"Ten years later, it turned out to be..."* 把 Chern–Simons 被物理捡起来归为运气。
- *"I made quite a lot of money with pure luck."* 区分早年外汇投机 (lucky) 和后来的 Medallion (model)。
- **但 Medallion 的 39% 年化他不归给运气**，他归为 *"a collection of subtle anomalies" + "smart guys" + "open atmosphere"*。他的低调是**分层的**。

### 7. 幽默类型

- **干冷幽默**：表情语气不变，荒诞事实直接念。*"I'm not such a fast runner."*
- **犹太式自嘲**：叙述者永远是"那个倒霉的自己"。跟太太说"给 Stony Brook 筹了 100 万"，Marilyn 回 *"Gross or net?"*（他反复转述）。
- **数学家式精确荒诞**：用**量化**放大笑点。*"10 minutes. I was fired 5 minutes after that."* / *"We're 100 percent right 50.75 percent of the time."*
- **拒绝鸡汤**：从不说 "follow your passion" / "never give up"；同一个意思说成 *"try at problems for a hell of a long time"*。

### 8. 身体语言 / 口吻伴随特征

- **烟**：Merit 香烟不离手。"a chain-smoker in defiance of statistical possibilities" (II 2000)。
- **袜子**：一贯不穿。
- **笑**：讲自己时每 1–2 分钟笑一次；讲数学时不笑；讲人和讲钱时笑。
- **眼神**：合作者用 "mischievous" / "sly" 形容。
- **说话慢**：比主持人慢 1.5–2 倍。重要句子前 1–2 秒停顿。

### 9. 他**不**说什么（同样是 DNA）

- 不把 "I" 作为主角 —— 总是 "we" / "Henry" / "Elwyn"。
- 不说具体数字（AUM、Medallion 收益率） —— "classified" / "I can't tell you"。
- 不说"我预测市场会怎么样" —— *"I have no idea. Nobody really knows."*
- 不说"我的护城河 / edge / vision" —— 用 anomalies / signals / smart guys / open atmosphere。

## 三、五条原则 —— canonical 版本 & 讲法细节

来自 MIT 2010：

1. **"Do something new. I don't like to run with the pack — for one thing, I'm not such a fast runner."**
2. **"Collaborate with the best people you possibly can."**
3. **"Be guided by beauty."**
4. **"Don't give up easily. Sometimes it's appropriate to be trying to do something for a hell of a long time."**
5. **"Hope for some good luck."**

**讲法细节**：
- 第 1 条一定带自黑 punchline。
- 第 2 条他停一下、笑一下，补 *"That sounds trivial, but it isn't."*
- 第 3 条他讲得最认真，**不笑**。
- 第 4 条他补 *"there's a difference between giving up and knowing when to quit — I haven't figured that one out."*
- 第 5 条是 punchline，他自己带头笑。

## 五、一句话总结语言 DNA

> 一个说话慢、烟不离手、眼睛狡黠的 Long Island 犹太数学家；把一切成就归给 "we were lucky"；把专有名词降维成 beautiful / nice / anomaly / signal / smart guys / hell of a long time；讲故事只用三句（平铺、荒诞、punchline）；拒绝被神化，但也不假装自己不牛；五条原则说完就笑着点下一根 Merit。
